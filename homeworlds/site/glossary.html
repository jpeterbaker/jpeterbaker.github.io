Homeworlds Glossary

My main Homeworlds page

Common Abbreviations

  • Cat
    Short for "catastrophe." Also used as a verb for causing a catastrophe.
  • Sac
    Short for "sacrifice."
  • SDG
    Super Duper Games, where you can play Homeworlds online by email
  • y1, b2, r3, etc.
    Shorthand used on SDG for small yellow, medium blue, large red, etc.

Definitions in Pyramid Arcade Rules

  • Demolition fleet
    Your opponent has only one large ship at home along with another piece of that color. Sacrifice a y3 to move two ships of that color to their home, call catastrophe, and move in a large ship. You are likely to win if you have a red to sacrifice somewhere.
  • Blurbird mistakes
    Allowing your entire home fleet to be destroyed in a single catastrophe
    • Sitting duck
      You have nothing but three ships of the same color in your home. Your opponent destroys you by moving in the fourth.
    • Easy Bluebird
      You have nothing but two ships of the same color in your home. Your opponent destroys you by sacrificing a y2 to move in two more ships of that color (each from 1 move away).
    • Tricky Bluebird
      You have nothing but two ships of the same color in your home. Your opponent destroys you by sacrificing a y3 to move in two more ships of that color (one from 1 move away and another from 2 moves away).
  • Investment
    Occupying a large piece with a green ship with the intention of eventually sacrificing the green piece and building the large system marker as a ship.
  • Doomsday machine
    Any ship at home, two large yellows, and six other ships: three each of your opponent's star colors, with the six ships one jump from your opponent's home. You can win in two moves by sacrificing the yellows and moving all the six ships to their home for catastrophes.
  • Factory
    A large green and at least one other green piece when there are no greens left in the bank
  • Green teleport
    Sacrificing a green to rebuild it in another system
  • Happy system
    A system with exactly one piece of each color
  • Keep-away
    Discovering a star rather than letting your opponent build the piece as a ship
  • Primary directive
    Defend the homeworld with these principles:
    • Keep a large there
    • Get a red when your opponent does
    • Diversify and minimize your home fleet
    • Once one star is destroyed, monopolize the other color
  • Squonking
    Sacrificing a ship to prevent its capture
    Andy also uses the term for trading a ship to block larger builds, see this Homeworlds Theater
  • Terraforming
    Sacrificing a yellow ship and discovering the piece as a star in the same move
  • Planetary defense system
    A red star in a home system
  • Universe sizes
    • Large universe
      Game where initial homeworlds are three moves away
    • Small universe
      Game where initial homeworlds are two moves away
    • Microverse
      Game where initial homeworlds are one move away (connected)
  • Gemini star
    Homeworld with two stars of the same size

Terms I'd Like People to Use

  • If I control a system in a large-universe game, then that system may be my
    • Colony
      if it is connected to my home and not my opponent's
    • Outpost
      if it is connected to my opponent's home and not my own
    • Island
      if it is connected to neither home
  • Local economy
    The collection of colors at a particular system. For example, if you move/trade away your last red ship in a system, you have lost the local red economy.
  • Andy's home
    The homeworld setup b2 r1 g3 (Andy's standard start)
  • Instafreeze
    The fastest possible economic freeze, explained here
  • Doomsweek machine
    Like a doomsday machine but slow-acting, explained here
  • Deterrent principle
    A red anywhere is a red everywhere, explained here
  • Concentration
    The number of pieces of the same color in a system. Decreasing concentration in your systems is typically good.
  • Primary directive
    Keep a large ship at home. Contrary to the Pyramid Arcade rules, I think this principle is so important that it alone should be called the Primary Directive.
  • Material
    The amount of ship power a player has. A player has a material advantage if they have more ships or larger ships. Very roughly, material can be measured as the total pip count of a player's ships. I myself usually use this modified material score:
    • each small ship = 1
    • each medium ship = 3
    • first large ship = 12
    • second large ship = 9
    • each additional large ship = 7
  • Spite
    Sacrificing a red to capture a red in the same system when opponent is attempting a red catastrophe
  • Dirty factory
    Sacrificing a g3 to quickly build the last three ships of another color, hopefully including at least one large.
